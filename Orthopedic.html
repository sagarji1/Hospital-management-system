Orthopedic

Orthopedics is a branch of medicine focused on the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of disorders and injuries related to the musculoskeletal system. This includes bones, joints, ligaments, tendons, muscles, and nerves. Orthopedic surgeons are specialized medical professionals who use both surgical and non-surgical methods to address a wide range of conditions, from fractures and sprains to degenerative diseases like arthritis and congenital abnormalities. They play a crucial role in helping patients regain mobility, alleviate pain, and improve overall quality of life through personalized treatment plans tailored to each individual's needs.

OUR SPECIALTIES

Emergency Medicine

A hospital must have a well-equipped emergency department capable of handling various medical emergencies, such as trauma cases, heart attacks, strokes, and other critical conditions.

Intensive Care Unit (ICU)

Critical care services for patients with severe and life-threatening conditions, requiring continuous monitoring and specialized interventions.

Cardiology

Diagnosis and treatment of heart-related conditions, including cardiac surgery and interventional cardiology procedures.

Neurology

Specialized care for disorders of the nervous system, including neurological examinations, diagnostics, and treatment for conditions such as stroke, epilepsy, and neurodegenerative diseases.
